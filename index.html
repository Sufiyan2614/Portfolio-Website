Non-Profit · Web Design

Taaqud Non-Profit Website

Redesigning a charitable organisation's digital presence to increase donor trust and simplify volunteer engagement.

View Live Site →

📋 Project Overview

RoleLead UI/UX Designer
Duration6 weeks
ToolsFigma, Adobe Illustrator
PlatformWeb (Responsive)

🎯 Business Challenge

Taaqud, a non-profit based in Oman, had a website that was visually outdated and difficult to navigate. Potential donors couldn't quickly understand the organisation's mission, and volunteers had no clear path to sign up. Leadership wanted a digital presence that communicated credibility and made charitable action frictionless.

🔍 Research & User Pain Points

I conducted stakeholder interviews and reviewed analytics on the existing site. Key findings:

  • Users left within 20 seconds because the mission statement was buried below the fold
  • The donation CTA was absent from the homepage — users had to hunt through navigation
  • Mobile experience was broken on smaller screens, losing a significant portion of traffic
  • No trust signals — no impact numbers, no team info, no transparent financials

🗺 User Flow

Land on Homepage
→
Understand Mission
→
See Impact Stats
→
Choose: Donate / Volunteer
→
Confirm & Thank You

✏️ Design Decisions

Hero above fold

Moved the mission statement and primary CTA to the very top of the page, ensuring clarity within 3 seconds of landing.

Trust section

Added an impact counter section (families helped, volunteers active, years running) to immediately establish credibility before asking for any action.

Dual-CTA architecture

Separated volunteer and donor journeys with distinct visual hierarchies — acknowledging these are different user intents requiring different paths.

Mobile-first rebuild

Completely rethought the layout for mobile with thumb-zone CTAs and a sticky header with a "Donate Now" shortcut.

📈 Outcome

↑ Clarity

Mission communicated within 3 seconds — validated through informal user walkthroughs post-launch

Mobile ✓

Fully responsive across all devices, correcting a broken mobile experience that existed previously

↑ Engagement

Volunteer sign-up form received higher early-stage interaction following the streamlined flow redesign

E-Commerce · Mobile App · Web

Safaqat Website & App

End-to-end design of an e-commerce platform — from buyer discovery to seller dashboards — across web and mobile.

View Live Site →

📋 Project Overview

RoleLead UI/UX Designer
Duration10 weeks
ToolsFigma, Framer
PlatformWeb + iOS/Android

🎯 Business Challenge

Safaqat needed a full digital ecosystem — a consumer-facing e-commerce site, a mobile app for buyers, and a corporate dashboard for sellers. The challenge was creating a coherent experience across three very different contexts: casual browsing, mobile shopping, and operational management.

🔍 Research & User Pain Points

Research included competitive analysis of regional e-commerce platforms and user interviews with both buyers and sellers:

  • Buyers — wanted fast product discovery, clear pricing, and a checkout process with fewer steps
  • Sellers — needed an inventory management system that didn't require technical training
  • Trust gap — first-time buyers were hesitant without visible reviews or seller credibility signals
  • Mobile parity — most traffic was mobile but the previous experience heavily deprioritised it

🗺 User Flow (Buyer)

Browse / Search
→
Product Detail
→
Add to Cart
→
Checkout
→
Order Confirmation

✏️ Design Decisions

Unified Design System

Built a single Figma component library covering web and mobile — ensuring visual consistency across all three products without duplicating work.

Progressive checkout

Reduced checkout from 6 steps to 3 by combining address + delivery and implementing smart defaults for returning users.

Seller onboarding flow

Designed a guided onboarding for new sellers with contextual tooltips and a checklist model — reducing the learning curve significantly.

Trust layer

Added seller badges, review counts, and a "Verified Seller" system to address buyer hesitation on first purchases.

📈 Outcome

3→1

Unified design system serving web, mobile app, and corporate dashboard from a single source of truth

↓ Friction

Checkout steps reduced, contributing to improved early-stage purchase completion observed after launch

↑ Seller NPS

Structured onboarding made the seller dashboard accessible to non-technical users without additional training

Healthcare · Mobile App

Find Doctor App

Designing a mobile app that helps patients find and book verified local doctors — reducing the friction between health concern and care.

View Figma Prototype →

📋 Project Overview

RoleUI/UX Designer (Solo)
Duration5 weeks
ToolsFigma
PlatformiOS & Android

🎯 Business Challenge

Many patients — especially in smaller cities — struggle to find verified, available doctors nearby. Existing solutions were cluttered with irrelevant listings, lacked real availability information, and made booking feel like a chore. The goal was to create an app that felt as simple as ordering food, but for healthcare.

🔍 Research & User Pain Points

User interviews (5 participants) and competitive analysis of existing health apps revealed:

  • Users distrusted unverified listings — they wanted to see credentials and patient reviews
  • Appointment booking required too many steps — most apps had 7+ screen flows
  • Finding specialists by symptom (not specialty name) was not supported anywhere
  • No post-appointment follow-up — users had no record of consultations in-app

🗺 User Flow

Enter Symptom / Specialty
→
Browse Doctor Profiles
→
Select Time Slot
→
Confirm Booking
→
Consultation Record

✏️ Design Decisions

Symptom-first search

Instead of asking users to know what specialty they need, the search bar accepts natural language symptoms and maps to relevant doctor categories.

Doctor trust card

Each profile shows: photo, verified badge, years of experience, patient ratings, and consultation fee upfront — no hidden information.

4-step booking

Reduced the booking flow to 4 screens: Search → Profile → Slot → Confirm. Calendar-style slot selection made availability immediately clear.

Consultation history

Added a "My Health" tab storing past appointments, prescriptions, and notes — turning a transactional app into a health companion.

📈 Outcome

4 steps

Booking flow reduced to 4 screens from the industry average of 7+, making the process faster and less overwhelming

WCAG AA

Healthcare context demanded accessibility — all colour contrasts, text sizes, and tap targets meet WCAG AA standards

↑ Clarity

Symptom-first search design validated positively in prototype testing — users found a relevant doctor within 30 seconds in sessions

How I Work

Cart abandonment, sign-up friction, and unclear CTAs kill revenue. Through heuristic audits, session analysis, and A/B-tested redesigns, I identify exactly where users drop and redesign those moments with precision.
